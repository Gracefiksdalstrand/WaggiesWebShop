Design Elements

    • #7A9648, This is an earthy tone that represents nature and its growth, this matches out goal to let dogs grow in life without anxiety
    • #9FBB73 This shade of green is a bit softer and represents harmony and the holistic approach we have at Waggies
    • #F1EB90 Yellow represents the optimistic approach and the joy we want to spread among dogs and their owners
    • #F3B664 This orange tone represents energy, the energy that we want to give the dogs by reducing their anxiety
    • #EC8F5E This warmer shade of orange represents friendliness that we want to embody, while also making people feel welcome
      • The reason we have to shades of green and orange is to have a smaller contrast that works well on our website. We use this for a color change when you hover over the color when used in a button.
  • Font choices:
    • We have chosen two fonts with great care to maximize readability and aesthetic appeal for our website, Posts, and documents as a whole. Montserrat Classic is our main font selection; We chose this because of its classic elegance and adaptability. Its sleek design and simple lines make it ideal for successfully communicating our brand message. Crimson Pro was our choice for a backup typeface. We think that this decision perfectly harmonizes with Montserrat Classic, giving our viewers a better overall visual experience. Crimson Pro is a great partner typeface because of its subtle elegance and readability, which ensure harmony and uniformity across our material.
  • User interface patterns:
    • We used grids and carousels to make things clearer. We have set up a navigation bar with clear and short words directing our website visitors to their preferred website as smoothly as possible. To make this even easier we also have a search icon located in the bar. Being on the home page immediately shows you a hero banner. With this, we aim to immediately grab the visitors' attention.

    Underneath this hero banner, we use variations of card layouts in a carousel. With this, we want to put our information in bite-size pieces for the viewers to now overwhelm them and still inform them about our brand and its ambassadors, products, and viewpoints.

    On every page, the navigation bar stays the same together with the footer. In the footer, we want to remind the viewer of important information like contact information, where we are located, a CTA button to sign up for our newsletter, and useful social media (Instagram). We made sure this was consistent for the whole website for a cohesive user experience. For signing up with the newsletter we tried to make it as easy as possible for the viewer to sign up by having a text box to type the email in with a button underneath to make the steps as small as possible which will hopefully result in a higher percentage of people finishing this ‚Äòtask‚Äô

    Across the whole website, we have made use of the ‚Äòhover effect‚Äô making the words or elements change color when hovering over it with the curser. We hope that this effect creates a more intuitive experience for our visitors while also giving more visual feedback which will create more engagement while also contributing to the aesthetics.
    We also have responsive designs that make it easy for the user to change the width and height of the website or use it on a phone, for instance, on the standard websites three products are displayed next to each other. While on the phone these are underneath each other and you have to scroll down to see them all.

    We also have implemented multiple feedback mechanisms on our website. We did this by giving people confirmation messages when they contact us or when they signed up for the newsletter. With these feedback mechanisms, we hope to provide the confirmation that will cause people to feel confident while using our website.

    We organized our content intuitively and naturally by making use of a very familiar layout for websites. Also, we used headings and subheadings that should make navigating and reading through our website easier. With this, we created categories, which gave us the possibility to put more than one topic on our home/index page.

Future Planning

If we were to continue the campaign, we would have to start by reevaluating how realistic our content calendar was, as we previously had both of our goals for reach, and the frequency of posting too high. We could adjust it so that it would become more realistic to execute (making sure to post regularly and not in a more spontaneous pattern), while remaining efficient for the Instagram and TikTok algorithms. We would be able to achieve this by making the posts and stories a week in advance, and using ‚ÄúAuto Publish‚Äù on Instagram so that we won‚Äôt forget to post them ourselves. 

 On TikTok, we learned that trend-hopping videos with green screen overlays did the most successful, so we would implement that into the content made for TikTok. As mentioned in the learning points, Facebook was initially going to be used, but due to some technical difficulties, we weren‚Äôt able to use it. If we were to continue the campaign, we would set up our Facebook account, and connect it to our Instagram. This way whatever is posted to Instagram would also be posted to Facebook, saving usfrom having to make different posts.

Our Planning would detail: Posting a story on Instagram every day, posting a TikTok 4 times a week (less frequently than the last planning), post a post to Instagram on Monday, Tuesday, and Friday between 9 a.m. and 1 p.m, and from midnight until 4 a.m (same as the last planning as they are the best days according to Sproutsocial, 2023).  

 

Our goal and message for the campaign would remain the same as it has aligned with our brand well.

Our objectives would be set for every 4 months (third) so that we can track the progress more frequently, to ensure we are evaluating and updating our methods when needed: 

  • By August 2024 we want to reach 2000 people/accounts via Instagram, and to get an average of 20 likes per post.
    • We have reached 567 people in less than a month, so this should be attainable.
  • By August 2024, we want to reach a 28.5% engagement rate on our Facebook page.
    • The average is 28.3%, (We Analyzed 2,810 Pages to Calculate Average Facebook Engagement Rate, iconosquare, 2022).
  • By August 2024, we aim to have reached 6500 people/accounts on TikTok.
    • With around 400 views per video, 4 times a week, for 4 months it gives a similar amount of people reached.
