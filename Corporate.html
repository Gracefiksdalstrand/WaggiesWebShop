  • Font choices:
    • We have chosen two fonts with great care to maximize readability and aesthetic appeal for our website, Posts, and documents as a whole. Montserrat Classic is our main font selection; We chose this because of its classic elegance and adaptability. Its sleek design and simple lines make it ideal for successfully communicating our brand message. Crimson Pro was our choice for a backup typeface. We think that this decision perfectly harmonizes with Montserrat Classic, giving our viewers a better overall visual experience. Crimson Pro is a great partner typeface because of its subtle elegance and readability, which ensure harmony and uniformity across our material.
  • User interface patterns:
    • We used grids and carousels to make things clearer. We have set up a navigation bar with clear and short words directing our website visitors to their preferred website as smoothly as possible. To make this even easier we also have a search icon located in the bar. Being on the home page immediately shows you a hero banner. With this, we aim to immediately grab the visitors' attention.

    Underneath this hero banner, we use variations of card layouts in a carousel. With this, we want to put our information in bite-size pieces for the viewers to now overwhelm them and still inform them about our brand and its ambassadors, products, and viewpoints.

    On every page, the navigation bar stays the same together with the footer. In the footer, we want to remind the viewer of important information like contact information, where we are located, a CTA button to sign up for our newsletter, and useful social media (Instagram). We made sure this was consistent for the whole website for a cohesive user experience. For signing up with the newsletter we tried to make it as easy as possible for the viewer to sign up by having a text box to type the email in with a button underneath to make the steps as small as possible which will hopefully result in a higher percentage of people finishing this âtaskâ

    Across the whole website, we have made use of the âhover effectâ making the words or elements change color when hovering over it with the curser. We hope that this effect creates a more intuitive experience for our visitors while also giving more visual feedback which will create more engagement while also contributing to the aesthetics.
    We also have responsive designs that make it easy for the user to change the width and height of the website or use it on a phone, for instance, on the standard websites three products are displayed next to each other. While on the phone these are underneath each other and you have to scroll down to see them all.

    We also have implemented multiple feedback mechanisms on our website. We did this by giving people confirmation messages when they contact us or when they signed up for the newsletter. With these feedback mechanisms, we hope to provide the confirmation that will cause people to feel confident while using our website.

    We organized our content intuitively and naturally by making use of a very familiar layout for websites. Also, we used headings and subheadings that should make navigating and reading through our website easier. With this, we created categories, which gave us the possibility to put more than one topic on our home/index page.

Testing Report

  • Testing Goals – The goal of testing our website WaggiesWebShop, was to see how our website was received by prospective clients, as well as making sure it was user friendly. We wanted to learn if a user was able to find everything that they would need to find to use our website.
  • Testing Methods, We conducted the test with six different people, who were within our target audience. All of these testers have experience with dogs, either though their own, parental, family or friend relations. The testers are:
    • Claudia Geelhoed Age 24
    • Bente Fuit Age 19
    • Annika Gabrielse Age 19
    • Noa van Bodegoem Age 20
    • Ollie Lita Age 21
    • Jesse van Baaren Age 21

      • The testing for our website was conducted both on a computer screen and on a Telephone screen. The Majority of the tests were conducted at the BUAS Campus, and one was conducted at Jesses house.
      Some of the tasks provided in the testing protocol were:

    • Can you please look through our home page and discover where the products page is
    • What are some of the products that we offer?
    • What is our mission at Waggies?
    • If you would have any questions about our company, or about the products that we offer, what would you do?
    • If you have clicked on products in the products that you want to purchase, where would you go to find these items?
    • We hope that you are more curious about our products and our company, so could you please look for our social media platform
    • Can you find the anti-slip paw wax
    • How do you feel about the UI/UX design of our website?
    • Is there anything you would improve about the WaggiesWebshop website experience?

      • These tests were recorded via iPhone and can be viewed here:https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1aRBKKpW607V3Xip_nBLtnA2_LVVLp96A?usp=drive_link

  • Testing Results

    Before conducting our Usability Testing, we informed the participants of our awareness of some issues with our website. Particularly the Ambassador photos missing and photos from our testimonials. In addition to this, we informed them of the overlapping text with video situation in the big hero box on the index page. These issues are something that our group is struggling to figure out how to fix, as our files are correct both on our computer and on Git Hub, as well as there are no mistakes in our code. Despite this, we still conducted user testability to the best of our abilities.

    These are some of the positive aspects of UI/UX identified from our testing interview:

    • Clear Product Visibility: The participant was able to identify the products page on the homepage, indicating effective presentation and visibility of products.
    • Effective Communication of Vision/Mission:The participant accurately identified the company's vision and mission, indicating that this information is effectively communicated on the website.
    • Accessible Contact Options: The participant knew how to contact the company for inquiries through various channels, such as filling out a form, emailing, or calling, indicating accessible communication options.
    • Positive Feedback on Design Elements: The participant praised various design elements of the website, including the navigation bar, colors, slideshow of products, and readability of content.


      • These are some of the negative aspects of UI/UX identified from the testing interview

    • UI/UX issues: The participant noted technical issues such as overlapping text and video, indicating potential problems with the website's display on certain devices or platforms
    • Unclear Product Presentation and product detail : Participants expressed confusion about where to find the products initially, suggesting a need for clearer presentation or labeling of product sections. The participants suggested that clicking on a product should provide more information or a larger image and the ability to add it to the cart, indicating a need for enhanced product presentation and functionality.
    • Issues with Photo Display: The participant noted issues with missing photos in sections like testimonials and ambassadors, suggesting potential problems with photo integration on the website.
    • Information Overload on Some Pages: The participant felt that certain pages had too much information, particularly when contact options were duplicated, suggesting a need for better organization or consolidation of information.
    • Text Legibility: The participant mentioned difficulty reading some text due to light font color, indicating a need for improved text contrast for better readability.
    • Suggestion for Cart Icon: The participant suggested using an icon instead of the word "Cart" for better visual appeal and consistency with other design elements.
    • Spelling and Formatting Issues: The participant noted some spelling errors and inconsistent formatting on the website, suggesting the need for proofreading and consistency in design elements.

      • Improvements

    • We would like to improve the responsiveness of the big hero box, so that it is responsive on a smaller screen, and doesnt remove the text.
    • In addition to this, for next time we would make it so that the products shown in the index page, were clickable, so it could take us directly to the products page.
    • We have improved spelling errors pointed out by testors, as well as some color correcting in certain areas of text.
    • If we had time, we would have liked to create a functioning cart.
    • On the corporate page, it looks like a lot of words are underlined, but despite our best efforts, were unable to remove this. This extends as well to the waggies webshop icon being the wrong color.

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Waggies campaign is about getting peopleâs attention and leading them to our website where we sell our calming essential oils, paw wax, and soaps for dogs, with our target audience being; Millennial dog owners, who are urban professionals, the two segments being double income no child, or high income with children and a dog. Our message to dog owners is that itâs possible to make their dogâs lives easier while also gaining more freedom for themselves. We conveyed this message by executing promotional activities through Instagram and TikTok. We posted videos, carousels, and stories on Instagram, and videos to TikToks, these followed trends to gain traction and awareness of our brand, and we shared our products, messages, and dog health tips.

Our objectives included; By April 2024 we want to reach 2000 people/accounts via Facebook, Instagram and TikTok. At the end of the campaign we want to achieve 2000 views on at least 1 TikTok and 50 likes on one Instagram post.

  • We picked Instagram, TikTok and Facebook as our platforms for promotion.     • Learning Points

    We learned that it takes more time than we first thought. We wanted to post 3 Instagram posts and a week, one TikTok daily, and stories on the days we donât post, but with all the other classes going on and demanding our attention, we often forgot about this and didnât meet the goal.

    We also struggled more with TikTok than expected, only after 9 TikTokâs we started gaining more than 800 views. Towards the end of the project we got our first TikTok with 1000+ views, making clear that our goal of 2000 views was set too high. Our most liked Instagram post got 34 likes, so we didnât reach our goal of 50 likes either. However throughout the process we discovered that we set the bar too high, so weâre still satisfied with the things we did manage to achieve so far.

    We also planned on using Facebook as a platform, but we got locked out of it and we had to send in a picture to get it restored. By the time we got our account back it already was too late to start a campaign on Facebook. Were the campaign longer we definitely wouldâve used Facebook since itâs big among millennials.

    Future Planning

    If we were to continue the campaign, we would have to start by reevaluating how realistic our content calendar was, as we previously had both of our goals for reach, and the frequency of posting too high. We could adjust it so that it would become more realistic to execute (making sure to post regularly and not in a more spontaneous pattern), while remaining efficient for the Instagram and TikTok algorithms. We would be able to achieve this by making the posts and stories a week in advance, and using âAuto Publishâ on Instagram so that we wonât forget to post them ourselves. 

     On TikTok, we learned that trend-hopping videos with green screen overlays did the most successful, so we would implement that into the content made for TikTok. As mentioned in the learning points, Facebook was initially going to be used, but due to some technical difficulties, we werenât able to use it. If we were to continue the campaign, we would set up our Facebook account, and connect it to our Instagram. This way whatever is posted to Instagram would also be posted to Facebook, saving usfrom having to make different posts.

    Our Planning would detail: Posting a story on Instagram every day, posting a TikTok 4 times a week (less frequently than the last planning), post a post to Instagram on Monday, Tuesday, and Friday between 9 a.m. and 1 p.m, and from midnight until 4 a.m (same as the last planning as they are the best days according to Sproutsocial, 2023).
